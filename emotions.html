A Note on Emotional Language

Words for emotions are approximations. They attempt to capture internal states that are often complex, layered, and contradictory. This dictionary seeks not to define absolutely, but to describe with nuance—acknowledging that what one person calls "sadness" might contain elements of relief, nostalgia, or peaceful acceptance.

Cultural context, personal history, and physiological state all shape how emotions are experienced and labeled. These entries are starting points for reflection, not final definitions.
